Its per-thread LOCAL frame is dominated by the lambda's reduced-Qinv `qr[kQpMaxM*kQpMaxM]` = 50*50 = 2500 doubles ≈ 20 KB (:1151), plus `xr[kQpMaxM]`/`xmat[kQpMaxM]` (:1112,:1147), `A/B/Ar/Br[kQpMaxT]` (:1118,:1128,:1161), and — because dev_als_weights inlines dev_seed_ab (which stacks `Vfull[kQpMaxNr*kQpMaxNr]`=100, `W[kQpMaxNl*kQpMaxNr]`=50 :188-192) and dev_opt_A/dev_opt_B (each `Wm[MAXM*MAXT]`=50*40=2000, `coeffs[MAXT*MAXT]`=1600 :228,235) — a deep nest of large local arrays. The file-top rationale (:33-39,:1085-1090) confirms this frame is sized to JUST fit launch at the SMALL bound, and the launch block is only 64 (:1358) precisely because the per-thread local/register pressure is so high. At the S8 rotation envelope (n_models in the millions) this single kernel does the most work with the largest per-thread frame in the unit, so register/local spill pressure here is the dominant occupancy limiter. NOT a correctness bug (the §12 op order + bit-parity are intact and the frame is documented-launchable). Suggested: candidate for splitting — the full-rank fit, the rank sweep, and the popdrop are independent passes that could be separate model-batched kernels (the LOO SE was already split out, see :1134-1138), each with a smaller frame ⇒ higher occupancy; the rank sweep in particular re-runs the whole als chain rmax+1 times in one thread and could be a (model, r) grid. Verify bit-parity (same seed/ALS/op order) after any split.
- [21.4][LOW] qpadm_fit_kernels.cu:1091 (qpadm_fit_models_kernel), :1198 (qpadm_loo_models_kernel), :832 (loo_large_batched_kernel), :922 (loo_batched_kernel) — None of the high-register per-thread-fanout kernels carries a `__launch_bounds__(maxThreadsPerBlock[, minBlocksPerSM])`. These kernels have large per-thread frames (the dev_als_weights / *_large nest) and are launched at hand-picked block=64 (:1358,:1547) / 128 (:1373) / 64 (:1494) with no compiler register budget hint, so the register allocator is free to use more registers than the chosen block can sustain at good occupancy (or to spill). A `__launch_bounds__` matching the launch block would let nvcc cap registers to the occupancy target deliberately. Complements Group 12 [12.4] (block size not derived from occupancy) from the register-hint side. Not a correctness issue. Suggested: add `__launch_bounds__(64)` (etc., matching each launcher's block) to qpadm_fit_models_kernel / loo_large_batched_kernel / loo_batched_kernel / qpadm_loo_models_kernel so the register budget is pinned to the intended occupancy; measure regs/occupancy with `-Xptxas -v` / ncu before/after.
- [21.4][LOW] qpadm_fit_kernels.cu:66-110 (dev_lu_factor/dev_solve), :118-176 (dev_jacobi_svd_V), :228-246,279-297 (dev_opt_A/B inner GEMM/build loops), :327-331 (dev_chisq_of quadratic form) — The tight inner LA loops have NO `#pragma unroll`. In the SMALL bucket the trip counts are small and effectively bounded (m<=50, t<=40, n<=nl<=5 in dev_solve; the host bucketer guarantees the envelope), so the dot-product / triangular-solve / Jacobi-rotation inner loops are good unroll candidates where the bound is tiny (e.g. dev_solve's back-substitution n<=kQpMaxNl=5, the per-(a,c) coeffs accumulation). Because the bounds are runtime (`m`,`t`,`n` params) the compiler will not fully unroll without the hint, and a deliberate `#pragma unroll` on the innermost fixed-tiny loops could cut loop overhead in the hottest single-thread LA. Apply DELIBERATELY only where the trip count is genuinely small and known-bounded — a reflexive unroll on the nb-length reductions (up to 757) would bloat code/registers and hurt. Not a correctness issue (unrolling preserves the FP64 op order). Suggested: add `#pragma unroll` to the innermost tiny-bound LA loops (dev_solve back/forward subst, dev_opt_A/B coeffs/rhs dot products) only; leave the nb-length jackknife reductions rolled.
